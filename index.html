Visa & Entry Details
Please provide details about your specific situation.
⚠️ Visitor Visa Overstay Risk Overstaying a visitor visa is a serious breach of immigration law. It may lead to removal, a re-entry ban, and affect future applications. You generally cannot switch to most other visas from inside the UK on a visitor visa.
🔴 Overstaying is a Serious Matter You have overstayed your leave to remain in the UK. This does not mean all routes are closed, but it is important to understand your situation accurately. Answer the questions below carefully.
10-Year Long Residence Route If you have lived in the UK continuously for 10 years — even with gaps or overstaying — you may qualify for ILR under the "Long Residence" rule. Absences must be no more than 18 months total and no single absence over 6 months. Legal advice is essential for this route.
🚨 Urgent — Seek Legal Help Immediately If removal proceedings are active, you may have very limited time to act. Contact an OISC-regulated advisor or immigration solicitor immediately. You may be able to appeal or apply for a stay of removal.
Dependant Details
Note on Children Born in the UK A child born in the UK to a parent with limited leave is not automatically British. However, once a parent gets ILR, the child can apply for ILR as a dependant and later for British citizenship if they meet the requirements.
Domestic Violence Concession / DVILR If your relationship broke down due to domestic violence, you may be eligible to apply for ILR immediately outside the normal qualifying period. Please seek specialist support.
✅ You Already Have ILR or Settled Status This is a strong foundation. The next steps typically involve either British citizenship (naturalisation) or maintaining your ILR. Let's gather a few more details.
Spouse of British Citizen — Reduced Qualifying Period If you're married to a British citizen, you may be eligible to apply for naturalisation after only 3 years of residence (instead of the standard 5 years), provided you have held ILR.
ℹ️ Asylum Claims Asylum law is highly complex and deeply personal. The information here is very general. We strongly recommend engaging with a specialist refugee law advisor or organisation such as Refugee Action, the Refugee Council, or a legal aid solicitor.

Residence History & Compliance
These questions apply to all applicants and help determine eligibility for long-term routes.
Long Absence Warning A single absence of over 6 months can break "continuous residence" for ILR purposes on most routes. However, certain exceptions exist (e.g. compelling overseas circumstances). Legal advice is important here.
🚨 Re-Entry Ban / Deportation History A previous removal or deportation order can result in a long-term bar on re-entry to the UK. The length depends on the circumstances. Specialist legal advice is essential.

🚨 Deception — Serious Consequences Using false documents or deception in a visa application can result in a 10-year ban from the UK. This is a complex area requiring specialist legal advice before any new application.
No Recourse to Public Funds (NRPF) Most visa holders in the UK are subject to NRPF conditions. Claiming benefits when subject to NRPF can affect current and future applications, and may be an immigration offence.
